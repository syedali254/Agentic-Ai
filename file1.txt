## Headache
A headache is pain or discomfort in the head or face area. It can be sharp, throbbing, or dull. Causes of headaches include stress, dehydration, lack of sleep, eye strain, sinus infection, or more serious issues like migraines and brain tumors.

### Causes:
- Tension (most common)
- Dehydration
- Sinus congestion
- High blood pressure
- Migraine (neurological origin)

### Remedies:
- Stay hydrated
- Take rest in a quiet room
- Use over-the-counter medicine like ibuprofen or acetaminophen
- For migraines: sumatriptan or other triptans

## Fever
Fever is a temporary rise in body temperature, often due to an illness. It's part of the body’s defense against infection.

### Symptoms:
- Body temperature above 100.4°F (38°C)
- Chills
- Sweating
- Body aches

### Common causes:
- Flu
- Infections (bacterial or viral)
- COVID-19
- Heat exhaustion

### Home care:
- Stay hydrated
- Rest
- Use antipyretics like paracetamol (acetaminophen) or ibuprofen
- Monitor temperature regularly

---

## Cold and Flu
Both are viral respiratory illnesses but caused by different viruses. Symptoms may overlap.

### Cold Symptoms:
- Sneezing
- Runny nose
- Mild cough
- Sore throat

### Flu Symptoms:
- Fever
- Body aches
- Extreme fatigue
- Dry cough
- Headache

### Prevention:
- Wash hands frequently
- Get vaccinated (especially for flu)
- Avoid close contact with sick individuals

## Diabetes
A chronic condition that affects the way the body processes blood sugar (glucose).

### Types:
- Type 1: Insulin-dependent, autoimmune
- Type 2: Lifestyle-related, insulin resistance
- Gestational: During pregnancy

### Symptoms:
- Frequent urination
- Excessive thirst and hunger
- Blurred vision
- Fatigue
- Slow wound healing

### Treatment:
- Healthy diet (low sugar, low carbs)
- Regular exercise
- Medication (metformin, insulin injections)

---

## Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
A condition where the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated. It increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.

### Symptoms:
- Often no symptoms (silent killer)
- Headaches
- Dizziness
- Blurred vision

### Causes:
- Genetics
- High salt intake
- Obesity
- Smoking
- Stress

### Control:
- Reduce sodium intake
- Exercise regularly
- Medication like amlodipine, lisinopril

---

## COVID-19
An infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Symptoms range from mild to severe.

### Common Symptoms:
- Fever
- Cough
- Shortness of breath
- Loss of taste or smell

### Prevention:
- Vaccination
- Masks in crowded places
- Hand hygiene
- Isolation when symptomatic

### Treatment:
- Supportive care (rest, fluids)
- Antivirals (in moderate to severe cases)
- Hospitalization in severe cases

---

## Antipyretic Medicines
Used to reduce fever.

### Examples:
- Paracetamol (acetaminophen)
- Ibuprofen
- Aspirin (not recommended for children with viral illness)

### Dosage (adults):
- Paracetamol: 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours (max 4g/day)
- Ibuprofen: 200–400 mg every 6–8 hours (max 1200 mg/day without prescription)

---

## Antibiotics
Used to treat bacterial infections. Not effective for viral illnesses like cold or flu.

### Common Types:
- Amoxicillin
- Azithromycin
- Ciprofloxacin
- Metronidazole

### Important Notes:
- Complete the full course
- Don’t self-prescribe
- Overuse can lead to antibiotic resistance

---

## Emergency Signs (Go to Hospital Immediately):
- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- High fever > 104°F (40°C)
- Severe vomiting or diarrhea
- Loss of consciousness
- Sudden weakness or numbness (possible stroke)
